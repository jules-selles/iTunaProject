Case

The Atlantic and Mediterranean Bluefin tuna (BFT) fishery has been considered the archetype of an over-fished and mismanaged fishery. This straddling stock (the Eastern stock spreads over the Mediterranean to the middle of the Atlantic) involves the cooperation between all the stakeholders (more than 10 fishing nations) in a Regional Fishery Management Organization (RFMO, the International Commission for the Conservation of Atlantic Tunas ICCAT). Every two years the Commission established through a voting process different kinds of regulations applicable to all stakeholders into their national management scheme (e.g. quota, season closure, gear restriction). All these decisions are based on a scientific advice promulgated by a Standing Committee on Research and Statistic (SCRS) which estimates the status of the resource and multiple predictions based on future harvested assumptions. This advice is uncertain, with process errors (poorly known biological processes: natural mortality due to changing environmental conditions; recruitment process; stock structuring…), and observations errors (e.g. misreported catch in length, effort…). These uncertainties alter the estimation of the stock status and the predicted impact of fishing mortality upon the stock, and therefore the predicted outcome expected by the fishery. Moreover, the fishery has experienced a brutal shift in there management regime in 2009 as a result of communications campaign from several NGOs which condemned together discrepancy between the scientific advice and the management decision and the high level of Illegal, Unreported and Unregulated fishing (IUU). The harvest level has been reduced roughly and the level of control has been increased to stop IUU fishing. This management shift had an economic impact on fleet involved in the exploitation of the Atlantic bluefin tuna.

The fishery is dominated by the purse seine fishing vessels which represent more than 60% of the catch realized from 8 nations (in 2013 for the purse seine fishery, data from task 1 of the ICCAT). This fishery is characterized by an international market with a strong demand for high quality tuna. The value chain is rather complex with a fattening phase in offshore farms strongly linked with the Japanese wholesalers. To have an abstract representation of this situation we represent {{ Constants.players_per_group }} similar stakeholders (with the same fleet) as coalitions of nations (European Union EU, distant-water fishing nations DWFNs and other coastal states (OCS)) which control the planning of the fishing harvest for their fishing industry (from one kind of fishing vessel). The Commission is also a simplified representation of the RMFO organization, in which representative of stakeholders (here the players) have to choose unilaterally (non cooperative way) their harvest level.

Our 2 main goals are to analyze decision making in a dynamic Common Pool Resource (CPR) dilemma when:
  • a critical resource size of different nature is introduced: non present, known with certainty and kown with uncertainty,
  • the resource size is known with uncertainty.
