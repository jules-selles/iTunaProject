Context

This experiment is concerned with the way stakeholders of international fisheries make collective decisions.
You will play as a representative and the manager of a Bluefin tuna fishing coalition of nations industry involved in the International Commission for the Conservation of Atlantic Tunas (ICCAT).
You will be assigned to a fishing coalition which has the same characteristics in term of fishing efficiency and capacity as others.
You will harvest the same stock of resource, the Bluefin tuna stock, with others nations controlled by a manager, a player, but you don't know who are these players and how they plan their fishing activity.
The game isn't not in a sequential nature like, in other words each year, each round you take a decision in term of abstraction from the resource, your decision more the others players decision will be felt in the next round.

Resource dynamic and Profit

The resource supports a dynamic which is known from players and shown through the ''Growth function'' given by .
This function gives you the growth potential of any stock level you left behind you.
The maximum growth is reached for a resource of {{ Constants.Bmsy }} (107 tones).
Each year, each round, you will select the level of catch allowable for your fishing industry.
You can select this catch from {{ Constants.min_catch }} to {{ Constants.max_catch }} (104 tones) , so each a maximum of {{ Constants.max_total_catch }} (104 tones) can be harvested.
The profit expected from this catch is linked to the state of the resource and can be deduced from the ''Profit functions''.
In view of this relationship, at the Maximum Sustainable Yield (MSY, the maximum harvest allowing a constant resource size) which represent a catch of {{ Constants.Ymsy }} (104 tones) per year , the total profit shared by all nations will be 25.1 (107 €).

Treatments

Depending on the treatment in which you are assiociated with, a critical level of biomass exists which determine an additional cost if the threshold Blim is exceeded.
This threshold represents a sudden shift in the management regime to mimic the socio-economic situation of the Atlantic bluefin tuna fishery.
The scientific committee of the ICCAT has an uncertain or a perfect (depending on the treatment) method to estimate the critical level of biomass.
This information is shared in the form of an accurate value of Blim or a range of values [Blim,min:Bmin,max].

Phases

Finally, during the experiment you will play 2 phases of this game with a different uncertainty on the resource state.
The accurate information about the resource size Bt will be deteriorate and you will have access only to the range of values [Bmin,t;Bmax,t] each year.
